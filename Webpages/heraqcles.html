Alkaline Electrolyzer Optimization

Advanced 3D multiphysics modeling for enhanced green hydrogen production efficiency

Project Overview

This research focuses on developing comprehensive 3D multiphysics models for alkaline water electrolyzers using COMSOL Multiphysics. The project integrates electrochemistry, fluid dynamics and species transport to optimize hydrogen production efficiency.

Through systematic parametric studies, the work identifies optimal operating conditions and design configurations that enhance performance by up to 30% compared to conventional systems.

Developed novel flow field designs and electrode configurations that significantly improve current distribution uniformity, reduce ohmic losses, and enhance overall system efficiency while maintaining cost-effectiveness for industrial applications.

Modeling activities

CFD Modeling of a Nanomesh Integrated with a Substrate

This study uses 3D CFD modeling to analyze the flow behavior within nanomesh structures integrated onto porous substrates. The simulations explore various geometric configurations to optimize fluid transport. The model incorporates detailed meshing of nanoscale pores and interconnecting channel. Boundary conditions are carefully defined to simulate realistic inlet velocities, pressure gradients, and fluid properties. Parametric analyses are conducted to assess the influence of pore size distribution, mesh thickness, and substrate porosity on flow uniformity.

Data post processing of BOP

Stable operation of industrial AWE hydrogen production systems depends on liquid level and pressure balance for efficient gas–liquid separation and high-purity hydrogen generation. Python supports data processing, mass balance modeling, prediction, and optimization to improve stability, degradation monitoring, and safety. Key parameters: HTO, OTH, I, V, L%, T, ∆P.

Innovative Flow Field Design

A novel 3D flow field design specifically engineered (driven by CFD simulation) to enhance fluid distribution and gas removal within alkaline water electrolyzers. This design ensures uniform flow velocity, minimizes bubble accumulation and detachment issues, and significantly improves mass transport efficiency at the electrode–electrolyte interface. It demonstrates up to 30% improvement in overall energy efficiency.

Shunt current calculation

This study models and analyzes shunt current, pressure drop, and manifold optimization in an alkaline water electrolysis stack using COMSOL Multiphysics, MATLAB/Simulink, and Python. The simulations evaluate how manifold geometry and electrolyte flow affect current leakage, pressure distribution, and overall stack efficiency. Results highlight the trade-off between large-cell designs, which increase bubble accumulation, and small-cell configurations, which elevate shunt current through multiple manifolds. The optimized design minimizes energy loss and ensures uniform gas evolution across the stack.

A multiphase transport model for the evaluation of electrode and PTL bubble Coverage in a Zero-Gap AWE

EThis study presents modeling results for a hydroxide exchange membrane (HEM) electrolyzer comprising a HEM, nanostructured electrodes, porous transport layers (foam), and fluid flow channels. The system is simulated using a multiphase COMSOL model validated by experimental data. The model represents a zero-gap HEM electrolyzer with an active area of 4 cm² and 11 parallel flow channels, evaluated across different flow rates to analyze fluid dynamics and electrochemical performance.

Key Findings

Design Optimization

  • Electrode Porosity: 70-80% porosity balances active surface area with electrolyte access
  • Flow Field Geometry: Novel serpentine designs improve uniformity and reduce dead zones
  • Inter-Electrode Gap: Optimized spacing minimizes ohmic losses while maintaining flow stability
  • Temperature: Optimal range of 60-80°C provides 15% efficiency gain compared to 40°C operation
  • Pressure: Elevated pressure (5-10 bar) reduces bubble size and improves current distribution uniformity
  • Electrolyte Flow: Optimized flow rates enhance bubble removal without excessive pumping costs

Technical Tools

COMSOL Multiphysics: Primary simulation platform for 3D multiphysics modeling (electrochemistry, CFD, heat transfer, species transport)

Python: Automation, data processing, and machine learning integration

Impact

Scientific Contributions

  • First comprehensive 3D model with full coupling of electrochemical, thermal, and hydrodynamic phenomena
  • Novel flow field designs achieving 15-18% efficiency improvements
  • Design guidelines for manufacturers to reduce development time and costs
