Alkaline Electrolyzer Optimization

Advanced 3D Multiphysics Modeling for Green Hydrogen Production

Oct 2022 – Present
KU Leuven, Belgium
Postdoctoral Research
2 Publications

Project Overview

This research project focuses on developing advanced three-dimensional multiphysics models of alkaline water electrolysis systems to optimize green hydrogen production. Using COMSOL Multiphysics, I've created comprehensive computational models that simulate the complex interplay of electrochemical reactions, mass transfer, heat transfer, and fluid dynamics within alkaline electrolyzers.

Research Objective

To enhance the efficiency and performance of alkaline water electrolyzers through computational optimization of flow field designs, electrode configurations, and operating conditions, contributing to more cost-effective green hydrogen production technologies.

Background & Motivation

Green hydrogen, produced through water electrolysis powered by renewable energy, is a cornerstone technology for the global energy transition. Alkaline electrolyzers offer advantages in terms of cost, durability, and scalability compared to other electrolysis technologies. However, optimizing their performance requires deep understanding of complex multiphysics phenomena.

Environmental Impact

Green hydrogen production eliminates CO₂ emissions from hydrogen generation, supporting global decarbonization efforts and renewable energy integration.

Economic Potential

Optimized alkaline electrolyzers can reduce the levelized cost of hydrogen production, making green hydrogen more competitive with fossil fuel alternatives.

Key Challenges

  • Mass Transfer Limitations: Bubble formation and accumulation can block active electrode sites and increase ohmic resistance
  • Non-Uniform Current Distribution: Poor flow field design leads to uneven current density and reduced overall efficiency
  • Heat Management: Temperature gradients affect reaction kinetics and can lead to performance degradation
  • Scale-Up Challenges: Laboratory-scale designs often don't translate efficiently to industrial-scale systems

Research Methodology

Computational Modeling Approach

I developed a comprehensive three-dimensional multiphysics model using COMSOL Multiphysics that couples multiple physical phenomena to accurately simulate alkaline electrolyzer performance.

Fluid Dynamics

  • Two-phase flow (liquid-gas)
  • Turbulence modeling
  • Bubble dynamics

Mass Transfer

  • Species transport
  • Diffusion-convection

  • Concentration profiles

Electrode Porosity Effects

  • Higher porosity (60-70%) improves mass transfer but reduces structural integrity
  • Optimal porosity identified at 65% balancing performance and mechanical strength
  • Graded porosity designs showed 12% improvement over uniform porosity
  • Pore size distribution critically affects bubble detachment and removal

Operating Condition Optimization

Systematic investigation of temperature, pressure, and current density revealed optimal operating windows:

Temperature Effects

  • Optimal range: 60-80°C
  • Higher temperatures improve kinetics
  • Balance with material degradation
  • 15% efficiency gain at 70°C vs 40°C

Pressure Effects

  • Elevated pressure reduces bubble size
  • Improved bubble removal efficiency
  • Better current distribution uniformity
  • Optimal at 5-10 bar operating pressure

Governing Equations

Key Mathematical Models

  • Butler-Volmer Equation: Describes electrochemical reaction kinetics at electrode surfaces
  • Navier-Stokes Equations: Governs fluid flow with two-phase considerations
  • Species Transport: Maxwell-Stefan diffusion with convection and migration
  • Charge Conservation: Ohm's law in both electrolyte and electrode phases
  • Energy Balance: Heat generation from reactions and ohmic losses

Computational Details

Mesh Statistics

  • Total elements: ~2.5 million
  • Element type: Tetrahedral
  • Boundary layer: 5 layers
  • Growth rate: 1.2

Computational Performance
