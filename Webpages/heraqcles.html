Alkaline Electrolyzer Optimization

Advanced 3D multiphysics modeling for enhanced green hydrogen production efficiency

Project Overview

This research focuses on developing comprehensive 3D multiphysics models for alkaline water electrolyzers using COMSOL Multiphysics. The project integrates electrochemistry, fluid dynamics and species transport to optimize hydrogen production efficiency. Through systematic parametric studies, the work identifies optimal operating conditions and design configurations that enhance performance by up to 30% compared to conventional systems.

Key Achievement

Developed novel flow field designs and electrode configurations that significantly improve current distribution uniformity, reduce ohmic losses, and enhance overall system efficiency while maintaining cost-effectiveness for industrial applications.

Key Findings

Design Optimization

  • Electrode Porosity: 70-80% porosity balances active surface area with electrolyte access
  • Flow Field Geometry: Novel serpentine designs improve uniformity and reduce dead zones
  • Inter-Electrode Gap: Optimized spacing minimizes ohmic losses while maintaining flow stability
  • Temperature: Optimal range of 60-80°C provides 15% efficiency gain compared to 40°C operation
  • Pressure: Elevated pressure (5-10 bar) reduces bubble size and improves current distribution uniformity
  • Electrolyte Flow: Optimized flow rates enhance bubble removal without excessive pumping costs

Technical Tools

COMSOL Multiphysics: Primary simulation platform for 3D multiphysics modeling (electrochemistry, CFD, heat transfer, species transport)

Python: Automation, data processing, and machine learning integration

Impact

Scientific Contributions

  • First comprehensive 3D model with full coupling of electrochemical, thermal, and hydrodynamic phenomena
  • Novel flow field designs achieving 15-18% efficiency improvements
  • Design guidelines for manufacturers to reduce development time and costs

Industrial Applications

  • Scale-Up: Guidelines for transitioning from lab-scale to industrial MW-scale systems
  • Cost Reduction: Optimized designs reduce material costs while improving performance
  • Sustainability: Enhanced efficiency directly lowers energy consumption per kg of H₂ produced
