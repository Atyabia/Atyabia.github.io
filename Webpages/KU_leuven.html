Alkaline Electrolyzer Optimization

Advanced 3D multiphysics modeling for enhanced green hydrogen production efficiency

Project Overview

This research portfolio focuses on developing comprehensive CFD models and simulations for advanced separation and recovery systems using COMSOL Multiphysics. The projects integrate electrochemistry, fluid dynamics, mass transport phenomena, and membrane science to optimize process efficiency for lithium recovery from spent batteries, protein separation via membrane chromatography, and mass transfer analysis in specialized devices.

Modeling activities

CFD Modeling of Electrodialysis System for Lithium-ion Recovery

This project focuses on CFD modeling of an electrodialysis (ED) system integrated with a Selective Cation Exchange Membrane (SCEM) for recovering lithium ions from black mass leachate. The simulations predict ion transport behavior, concentration gradients, and process efficiency under varying operational conditions. The model validates the selective extraction of Li⁺ ions while minimizing interference from competing ions. Key findings support the development of scalable ED systems for circular battery material recovery, with applications in lithium-ion battery recycling and sustainable resource management.

CFD-Optimized Membrane Chromatography for Protein Separation

Comprehensive CFD modeling of membrane chromatography systems using both spiral and conical membrane module geometries for protein separation applications. The simulations optimize flow distribution, residence time, and mass transfer efficiency within the membranes. Advanced CFD insights led to improved module designs that were validated through 3D printing and experimental testing. A leak-free opening design was engineered specifically for conical modules to enable scalable implementation. The work demonstrates how computational fluid dynamics can accelerate the design-to-prototype cycle for bioprocess separation systems.

2D/3D CFD Analysis of Mass Transport in an osmotic pump device

Detailed computational study of mass transfer phenomena in the osmotic pump device through coupled 2D and 3D CFD simulations in COMSOL. An analytical 0D model was developed and rigorously compared with 2D CFD predictions to validate transport mechanisms. Multiple CFD configurations were created to isolate and examine different aspects of transport behavior including diffusion, convection, and reaction kinetics. The analysis identified critical performance bottlenecks and proposed model improvements based on comprehensive data evaluation. This multi-scale modeling approach provides detailed insight into device performance and informs optimization strategies.

Achivments

Design Optimization

  • Nanomesh-Substrate Integration: 3D CFD modeling optimized pore size distribution and mesh thickness, enhancing fluid transport uniformity by 25%
  • BOP Data Analytics: Python-based mass balance modeling improved stability monitoring and degradation prediction through real-time analysis of HTO, OTH, and pressure parameters
  • 3D Flow Field Innovation: Novel flow field design demonstrated 30% energy efficiency improvement through uniform velocity distribution and minimized bubble accumulation
  • Shunt Current Optimization: Multiphysics modeling identified optimal manifold geometry, reducing energy loss from current leakage while balancing cell size trade-offs
  • Zero-Gap HEM Electrolyzer: Multiphase transport model for 4 cm² active area system validated bubble coverage dynamics across 11 parallel channels at varied flow rates
  • Gas Crossover Mitigation: 3D CFD models quantified membrane transport mechanisms, optimizing separator properties to minimize hydrogen-oxygen mixing under operating conditions
