Electrolysis with Cogeneration of Ca(OH)₂

CFD Simulation and Membrane Selection for Decarbonization of Lime Manufacturing

Project Overview

Electrolysis with cogeneration of Ca(OH)₂ is considered as a possible option for the electrification of the lime manufacturing process. This project covers the identification of suitable membranes through process calculations and numerical simulation, combined with literature review to identify suitable existing membrane materials for testing.

CFD Modeling

Computational fluid dynamics simulations were performed using a 2D COMSOL Multiphysics® model to study mass transport, electric charge distribution, and electrochemical reactions within the electrolysis cell. The modeling successfully evaluated two process configurations. These simulations revealed key design parameters affecting energy consumption and informed the recommendation to adopt the configuration.

Key Findings

Modeling shows that thinner membranes with higher porosity and higher ion exchange capacity (IEC) are preferred for their lower energy consumption.

Membrane Selection

The membrane inventory search revealed several membranes considered suitable for the application.

Recommended Configuration

For enhanced energy efficiency and higher precipitation concentration, we suggest adopting a novel configuration.

Project Outcome

The use of novel concept can be further beneficial as it allows casting of tailored membranes (thickness, IEC, porosity).
