About Me

Ph.D. mechanical engineer with 10+ years of expertise in multiphysics CFD simulations for electrochemical systems. Proven track record of delivering computational solutions for electrodialysis, water electrolyzers, fuel cells, and membrane systems that achieve measurable performance improvements (up to 30% efficiency gains). Strong background in optimizing industrial processes through advanced modeling, with extensive experience collaborating across academic and industry environments including VITO, KU Leuven, imec, and John Cockerill.

Research Focus

My work bridges theoretical modeling and experimental validation in electrochemical energy systems. I develop detailed CFD models to optimize fuel cell performance, improve electrolyzer efficiency, and advance battery thermal management solutions. Currently focused on alkaline water electrolyzers for green hydrogen production, CO2 electrolyzers for sustainable carbon capture, and electrodialysis systems for advanced separation processes.

HERAQCLES - AEM Electrolyzer

VITO | 2021 - 2024

HERAQCLES advances anion exchange membrane (AEM) electrolysis by developing innovative components and design-for-manufacturing architecture. Designed and simulated a novel round-shaped flow field achieving 30% energy savings. Developed Python-based tools for Balance of Plant (BOP) analysis and shunt current calculation.
