About Me

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at University Name. My research focuses on artificial intelligence, machine learning, and their applications in real-world problems. I am particularly interested in deep learning, natural language processing, and computer vision.

Before joining University Name, I completed my PhD at MIT, where I worked on developing novel neural network architectures for image recognition. I have published over 30 papers in top-tier conferences and journals including NeurIPS, ICML, CVPR, and Nature Machine Intelligence.

Research Areas

🧠 Deep Learning

Developing novel neural network architectures and training methods for improved performance and efficiency. Focus on transformer models, attention mechanisms, and efficient training strategies.

💬 Natural Language Processing

Working on language understanding, generation, and multimodal learning. Applications include machine translation, sentiment analysis, and question-answering systems.

👁️ Computer Vision

Research on image recognition, object detection, and semantic segmentation. Exploring applications in medical imaging, autonomous vehicles, and augmented reality.
